What is One Person Company Registration?

A One Person Company (OPC) is a business structure introduced under the Companies Act, 2013, that allows a single Indian citizen and resident to form a private limited company. It gives the sole owner full control while offering limited liability, separate legal status, and perpetual succession. OPCs must appoint a nominee who will take over if the owner dies.

Combining the simplicity of a sole proprietorship with limited liability, an OPC has a single owner who acts as both shareholder and director, with a nominee appointed for succession. Initiated by a promoter, it is a separate legal entity that can own assets and enter into contracts.

Key features of a One Person Company in India

An OPC is designed to combine the simplicity of sole proprietorship with the benefits of a company structure. Key characteristics include:

  • Single Member Ownership: An OPC is formed and owned by a single individual who is both the shareholder and director, making it ideal for solo entrepreneurs.
  • Separate Legal Entity: The OPC is a distinct legal entity, separate from its owner, meaning the company can own assets and enter into contracts in its own name.
  • Limited Liability: The owner’s liability is limited to the extent of their shareholding, protecting personal assets from business debts and liabilities.
  • Perpetual Succession: The company continues to exist even if the owner dies or becomes incapacitated, with a nominee stepping in automatically.
  • Nominee Requirement: A nominee must be appointed at the time of registration to take over in case the sole member can no longer manage the company.
  • Restricted Share Transfer: Shares cannot be freely transferred to outsiders; the sole member holds complete ownership until the company is converted into a private or public limited company.

Key Laws & Regulations Governing One Person Companies in India

Understanding the legal framework is crucial. One Person Company registration and operation in India are primarily governed by a comprehensive set of laws:

  • Companies Act, 2013: The primary legislation governing all aspects of company formation, operation, dissolution, and corporate governance standards.
  • Income Tax Act, 1961: Regulates the taxation aspects of private limited companies, including corporate tax rates, deductions, and filing obligations.
  • Goods and Services Tax (GST) Laws: Mandatory GST registration is required for companies crossing specified turnover thresholds.
  • Information Technology Act, 2000: Applies to companies engaging in electronic commerce and digital business activities.

Key Regulatory Authorities for One Person Companies

Your journey to establish and operate a One Person Company involves interaction with several critical government bodies:

  • Registrar of Companies (ROC): Under the Ministry of Corporate Affairs, the ROC processes your SPICe+ application and issues the Certificate of Incorporation.
  • Income Tax Department: Manages corporate tax filings and direct tax aspects.
  • Goods and Services Tax (GST) Council: Oversees indirect tax compliance if your turnover exceeds the GST threshold.

Types of One Person Company (OPC) Registration in India

Before proceeding with OPC registration, it's important to understand the different types available to choose the one that best suits your business needs:

  • OPC Limited by Shares: In this type of OPC, the liability of the single member is limited to the unpaid amount on the shares they hold. Once the shares are fully paid, the member has no further financial obligation towards the company’s debts. This is the most common type of OPC in India.
  • OPC Limited by Guarantee: In this structure, the member’s liability is limited to the amount they agree to contribute in the event of the company being wound up. This amount is specified in the Memorandum of Association (MoA) and is typically used for non-profit activities.
  • Unlimited OPC: In an Unlimited OPC, the member has no limit on their liability. This means that if the company is unable to meet its financial obligations, the member may be required to use their personal assets to clear the debts. This type of OPC is very rare due to the high financial risk involved.

Eligibility Criteria for OPC Registration in India

To register a One Person Company (OPC) in India, you must meet certain criteria as per the Companies Act, 2013:

  • Citizenship and Residency: The person forming the OPC must be a natural person, an Indian citizen, and a resident of India. A resident is someone who has lived in India for at least 120 days in the preceding financial year.
  • Single Directorship: An individual can be a director in only one OPC at a time. This restriction ensures that the focus remains on a single venture under the OPC structure.
  • Age Requirement: The promoter must be at least 18 years of age and legally competent to enter into a contract.
  • Nominee Requirement: A nominee must be appointed, who must also be an Indian citizen and resident. The nominee will take over the company in case of the promoter’s death or incapacitation.
  • Turnover and Capital Limits: An OPC cannot be converted into a public or private company unless its paid-up share capital exceeds ₹50 lakhs or its average annual turnover for the last three consecutive years exceeds ₹2 crores. If these limits are crossed, mandatory conversion to a private limited company is required.

Documents Required for OPC Company Registration

To ensure a smooth OPC registration process, you’ll need to prepare the following documents:

For the Director & Nominee

  • PAN Card (Mandatory)
  • Aadhar Card/Driving License/Passport
  • Recent passport-sized photograph
  • Address Proof (Latest utility bill or bank statement, not older than 2 months)
  • Email ID & Mobile Number
  • Specimen signature

For the Registered Office Address

  • Proof of Address: Latest Utility Bill (not older than 2 months) or Property Tax Receipt.
  • No Objection Certificate (NOC): From the property owner.
  • Rent/Lease Agreement: (if applicable)
